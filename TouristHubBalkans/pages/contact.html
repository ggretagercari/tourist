Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common travel questions

What is the best time to visit Kosovo and Albania?

The best time to visit is during spring (April-June) and autumn (September-October) when the weather is pleasant and crowds are smaller. Summer is ideal for coastal areas in Albania, while winter offers skiing opportunities in the mountains.

Do I need a visa to visit Kosovo and Albania?

EU citizens, US, UK, Canada, and many other countries can enter visa-free for up to 90 days. Please check current visa requirements based on your nationality before traveling.

What currencies are used?

Kosovo uses the Euro (EUR), while Albania uses the Albanian Lek (ALL). Euros are widely accepted in Albania, especially in tourist areas. Credit cards are accepted in major establishments.

Is it safe to travel in Kosovo and Albania?

Both countries are generally very safe for tourists. Crime rates are low, and locals are known for their hospitality. Standard travel precautions apply, especially in crowded areas.

What languages are spoken?

Albanian is the main language in both countries, with Serbian also official in Kosovo. English is increasingly spoken in tourist areas, and German and Italian are common due to diaspora connections.

How do I get around between countries?

Regular bus services connect major cities. Car rental is available, and the road network is generally good. Flights connect to major European cities. Border crossings are straightforward with proper documentation.
